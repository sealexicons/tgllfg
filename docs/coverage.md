## What's in each section

### voice/aspect (294 sentences, 100%)

Voice × aspect cross-product over the 6 anchor verbs (kain, bili,
basa, sulat, gawa, tapon) for AV/OV/DV/IV × PFV/IPFV/CTPL where
the verb participates in that voice. Plus the 3 intransitive AV
verbs (takbo, tulog, uwi) across PFV/IPFV/CTPL. Phase 4 §7.1 +
§7.2 deliverables. Plus the Phase 5e Commit 14 mang- retain
fixtures (3 sentences: bili PFV/IPFV/CTPL retain-pattern parses).

### control (105 sentences, 100%)

Three control patterns from §7.6: psych (gusto/ayaw/kaya with GEN
experiencer), intransitive (pumayag), transitive OV/DV (pinilit,
inutusan). Plus negation composition (outer + inner). Plus the
Phase 5e Commit 8 control / raising composition fixtures
(9 sentences: control under linked raising mukha/baka, control
under bare raising parang/tila, TRANS control + raising in XCOMP,
NEG composition at middle / inner level). Plus the Phase 5e
Commit 9 nested-control composition fixtures (8 sentences:
nested pa-OV under control across psych+intrans / intrans+psych /
psych+psych orderings, 3-arg pa-OV under nested, IV multi-GEN
under nested, NEG at innermost / middle level).

### clitic (91 sentences, 100%)

Wackernagel 2P clitic placement from §7.3: pronominal NOM/GEN
clitics, adverbial enclitics (na/pa/ba), pron + adv combinations.
Plus the Phase 5e Commit 20 ``kita`` fusion fixtures
(9 sentences): bare frames across OV (``Kinain kita``), DV
(``Binasahan kita``), IV-BEN (``Ipinaggawa kita``), and pa-OV
(``Pinakain kita``); 3-arg with-PATIENT frames
(``Sinulatan kita ng liham``, ``Pinakain kita ng kanin``,
``Ipinaggawa kita ng silya``); negation (``Hindi kita kinain``,
with kita hoisted to the post-V cluster); and adv enclitic
co-occurrence (``Kinain na kita``).

### causative (88 sentences, 100%)

§7.7 monoclausal direct causative (`pa-...-in` OV) and biclausal
indirect causative (`magpa-` AV). 3 verb roots (kain, basa, inom)
× 3 aspects each, plus argument variations. Plus the Phase 5e
Commit 10 3-arg pa-DV (`pa-...-an` with overt PATIENT) fixtures
(4 sentences: 3 NP-order permutations + basa anchor). Plus the
Phase 5e Commit 11 3-arg plain DV (`-an` with AGENT, PATIENT,
RECIPIENT) fixtures (4 sentences: 3 NP-order permutations + IPFV
aspect; sulat anchor). Plus the Phase 5e Commit 12 mag-...-an
reciprocal / social fixtures (5 sentences: PFV/IPFV/CTPL kain,
PFV bili, NEG composition).

### ay-inversion (85 sentences, 100%)

§7.4 SUBJ-fronted ay-inversion across voices, aspects, and with
negation composition. Plus the Phase 5e Commit 1 follow-on
pa-OV / pa-DV (CAUS=DIRECT) actor-fronting fixtures (8 sentences:
2-arg pa-OV across kain/basa/inom, 3-arg pa-OV with overt OBJ-PATIENT,
2-arg pa-DV across kain/basa/inom, plus an inner-NEG case). Plus
the Phase 5e Commit 2 multi-GEN-NP ay-fronting fixtures
(8 sentences: IV-BEN/IV-INSTR/IV-REASON 3-arg with AGENT
fronted, IV-BEN/IV-INSTR 3-arg with PATIENT fronted, pa-OV-direct
3-arg with PATIENT fronted (kain/basa), plus an inner-NEG case).
Plus the Phase 5e Commit 3 AdvP / PP ay-fronting fixtures
(10 sentences: temporal AdvP with kahapon/ngayon/bukas/mamaya,
AdvP with NEG and transitive inner, PP fronting across the four
seeded prepositions para/tungkol/mula/dahil).

### relativization (89 sentences, 98%)

§7.5 SUBJ-only relativization with both `-ng` (vowel-final hosts)
and `na` (consonant-final hosts) linkers. **2 fragments** are
intentional: the `*ang batang kinain ang isda` pattern (attempting
to relativize a non-pivot OBJ) is correctly rejected at the
full-parse level but produces sub-span fragments — Tagalog's
SUBJ-only restriction is a hard syntactic constraint and we
preserve the fragments for diagnostic visibility. Plus the Phase
5e Commit 18 possessive-linker RC fixtures with pronominal
possessors (13 sentences): 4 consonant-final pronouns ``namin`` /
``natin`` / ``ninyo`` / ``nila`` in SUBJ position, OBJ-position
case, inner-NEG case, 3 vowel-final pronouns ``ko`` / ``mo`` /
``niya`` with the standalone ``na`` linker variant, consonant-
final HEAD (``kapatid namin na binasa``), DV variant
(``binasahan``), IV bare + IV with DAT adjunct (``ipinaggawa`` /
``ipinagsulat sa kapatid``). Plus the Phase 5e Commit 19 fixtures
with non-pronominal possessors (7 sentences): common-noun
possessors (``libro ng batang binasa``, ``libro ng asong
kinain``), proper-noun possessors with ``ni`` (``libro ni Juan
na binasa``, ``libro ni Maria na binasa``), OBJ-position case,
inner-NEG case, and IV variant — all using the same dual-binding
wrap rule generalized from PRON to NP[CASE=GEN].

### applicative (46 sentences, 100%)

§7.7 `ipag-` benefactive applicative (APPL=BEN), plus the
Phase 5c §7.7 follow-on `ipang-` instrumental applicative
(APPL=INSTR) and `ika-` reason applicative (APPL=REASON).
The `ipang-` cells use the new `nasal_assim_prefix` sandhi op
(homorganic-nasal place-assimilation with consonant retention),
distinct from the older `nasal_substitute` (drop pattern) which
the AV distributive `mang-` continues to use.

### negation (40 sentences, 100%)

§7.2 `hindi` declarative and `huwag` imperative negation across
voices and aspects.

### demonstrative (44 sentences, 100%)

§7.8 standalone demonstrative pronouns (ito/iyan/iyon and their
GEN counterparts nito/niyan/niyon) across NOM and GEN positions.
Plus the Phase 5e Commit 16 pre-modifier demonstrative fixtures
(13 sentences: PROX with bound `-ng` linker, MED / DIST with
standalone `na` linker, across NOM / GEN cases, plus a pre +
post-modifier stacking case `itong batang ito`). Plus the Phase
5e Commit 17 dem-on-relativized-head fixtures (10 sentences:
post-mod dem combined with a relative clause across PROX / MED
/ DIST, both linker variants on the dem, transitive and
intransitive RC bodies, GEN-headed in OV-actor position, and a
pre-mod + RC composition pin).

### possessive (18 sentences, 100%)

§7.8 NP-internal `ng`-NP possessor on SUBJ and OBJ positions.

### classic (12 sentences, 100%)

Hand-authored canonical examples drawn from the linguistic
literature (Schachter & Otanes 1972; Kroeger 1993). Sources are
recorded per-sentence in the corpus YAML.

### rg — Ramos & Goulet 1981 (20 sentences, 100%)

Real-world example sentences mined from the OCR'd companion
.txt of *Intermediate Tagalog: Developing Cultural Awareness
Through Language* (Teresita V. Ramos & Rosalina Morales Goulet,
University Press of Hawaii, 1981) under
``data/tgl/dictionaries/``. Filtering kept only sentences whose
vocabulary is fully covered by the seed lexicon and that the
Phase 4 grammar parses successfully. This satisfies the §7.10
plan-text "drawn from a Tagalog reference grammar"; expanding
to harder real-world coverage (vocabulary outside the seed,
constructions outside §7.1-§7.9) is a Phase 5+ enrichment.

Sentences span a representative slice of constructions: AV/OV
inflection across PFV/IPFV/CTPL aspects; questions with
adverbial enclitics (``na``, ``ba``); negation; ay-inversion;
NVOL morphology (``naka-`` / ``ka-``-form ``nakita``,
``nauhaw``). Page-level cross-reference is deferred — the OCR
loses page boundaries and reconstructing them from the PDF's
physical layout is its own task.

### quantifier (12 sentences, 100%)

§7.8 floated quantifier `lahat` / `iba` across intransitive and
transitive clauses.

### robustness (4 sentences, 0%)

§7.9 robustness — these are **intentional partial / failure
cases** to verify the fragment-extraction pipeline:

- `Kumain ng aso ang.` → 1+ fragments (incomplete NP after `ang`).
- `Kumain xyzqwerty ng aso.` → fragments (garbage word stripped,
  but residue lacks NOM-NP for full parse).
- `Aso ang isda.` → fail (no V → no rule predictions, no
  fragments either; expected behaviour).
- `Kumain ng.` → fail (`ng` without N → no completed NP states).

## What's intentionally **not** covered

The corpus reflects the implementation's documented out-of-scope
list (see `docs/analysis-choices.md`):

- **Long-distance relativization** through XCOMP / COMP (needs
  functional uncertainty in the unifier — §7.5 + §7.6 deferral).
- **Resumptive pronouns in RCs.** Reclassified to genuinely
  v1-out-of-scope in Phase 5e Commit 7 — Tagalog uses voice
  alternation, not resumptive pronouns, as the canonical
  strategy for non-SUBJ relativization (Kroeger 1993 §5; S&O
  1972 §5.16). The construction is not corpus-attested in a
  stable form; revisit if a resumptive-rich corpus emerges.
  See plan §18 and the analysis-choices.md "Phase 5e Commit 7"
  entry.
- **``magpa-...-an`` distributive.** Reclassified in Phase 5e
  Commit 13 (originally a Group D speculative entry). The
  attested AV-distributive marker in Tagalog is the existing
  ``mang-`` AV affix class (already implemented). ``magsi-``
  is also attested as a collective-distributive (S&O 1972
  §5.27) but not exercised by the corpus. See plan §18 and
  the analysis-choices.md "Phase 5e Commit 13" entry.
- **Other ``ipang-`` senses (purpose nominals).** Reclassified
  in Phase 5e Commit 15 (originally a Group D entry).
  ``pang-`` derives purpose nominals (``pambili`` "for
  buying", ``pansulat`` "writing instrument") from verbal
  roots — but these are nouns, not verbs, and the seed corpus
  doesn't exercise them. The verbal ``ipang-`` IV-INSTR
  applicative is already covered by Phase 5c §7.7 Commit 4;
  noun-deriving uses remain deferred. See plan §18 and the
  analysis-choices.md "Phase 5e Commit 15" entry.
- **DV PFV paradigm coverage gap (TBD).** Surfaced during
  Phase 5e Commit 18 review. The §7.1 DV PFV paradigm has
  full analyzer coverage only for ``binasahan`` (DV PFV of
  ``basa``); other DV PFVs (``binigyan`` of ``bigay``,
  ``binilhan`` of ``bili``, ``kinanan`` of ``kain``, …)
  analyse as ``_UNK``. The grammar admits these forms in
  multiple constructions (Phase 5e Commit 18 dual-binding,
  3-arg DV, DV under control, DV in standard relativization)
  but speakers only have ``binasahan`` to exercise them.
  Lifting requires expanding ``data/tgl/paradigms.yaml`` (the
  DV ``-an`` PFV cell) or hand-authored lex entries; the
  scope crosses many constructions so it's tracked as a
  paradigm-engineering follow-up rather than a single-
  construction lift. See plan §18 entry "DV PFV paradigm
  coverage gap".

### Recently lifted (still listed for archaeological context)

- **Other ``ipang-`` senses (reclassification)** — Phase 5e
  Commit 15 documentation-only commit. Originally listed as a
  Phase 5e Group D candidate ("additional senses can land as
  separate lex entries"). Investigation showed ``pang-``
  purpose nominals (``pambili``, ``pansulat``, ``pangkain``)
  are noun-deriving forms, not verbal voice / applicative
  variants. The verbal ``ipang-`` IV-INSTR use is already
  handled by Phase 5c §7.7 Commit 4; noun-deriving uses are
  reclassified to plan §18 v1-out-of-scope (lex-only path or
  productive-derivation path both deferred until corpus
  pressure surfaces specific tokens). No grammar / lex /
  morph / corpus changes.
- **AV mang- retain readings** — lifted in Phase 5e Commit 14.
  Schachter & Otanes 1972 §5.27 + Ramos 1971 paradigm tables
  document a retain-pattern variant of the ``mang-``
  distributive AV alongside the default drop pattern. The
  retain pattern keeps the base's first consonant and adds a
  homorganic nasal (the same ``nasal_assim_prefix`` op
  introduced for Phase 5c §7.7 ipang- IV-INSTR / ika-
  IV-REASON applicatives). New ``mang_retain`` affix class
  with PFV / IPFV / CTPL paradigm cells; per-base flagging via
  ``affix_class: [mang_retain]`` in roots.yaml. Currently only
  ``bili`` carries the flag — ``nambili`` / ``nambibili`` /
  ``mambibili`` parse alongside the existing ``namili`` /
  ``namimili`` / ``mamimili`` drop forms; both routes to the
  same ``BUY <SUBJ>`` lex PRED. Other roots (tahi, patay)
  could opt in via affix-class registration.
- **``magpa-...-an`` distributive (reclassification)** —
  Phase 5e Commit 13 documentation-only commit. Originally
  listed as a Phase 5e Group D candidate ("distributive
  causative variant"); investigation showed it has no clear
  attestation in S&O 1972, Ramos 1971, or R&B 1986. Tagalog
  AV-distributive is the existing ``mang-`` affix class (already
  implemented); ``magsi-`` is a separate attested collective-
  distributive form not exercised by the corpus. Reclassified
  to plan §18 v1-out-of-scope. No grammar / lex / morph / corpus
  changes.
- **mag-...-an reciprocal / social** — lifted in Phase 5e
  Commit 12. The plan §10.1 Group D originally flagged
  ``ka-...-an`` as the "reciprocal causative", but
  investigation against S&O 1972 + Ramos 1971 + R&B 1986
  confirmed that the well-attested reciprocal pattern is
  ``mag-...-an`` (``ka-...-an`` is more typically a
  noun-deriving pattern). New ``mag_an`` affix class with
  PFV/IPFV/CTPL paradigm cells; lex entries ``EAT-TOGETHER``
  for kain reciprocal and ``BUY-EXCHANGE`` for bili reciprocal,
  with discriminator ``MOOD=SOC`` (lifting the Phase 4 §7.2
  SOC-as-inventory-only deferral). The plain AV-intr lex
  entry's lack of MOOD constraint produces a known ambiguity
  (``Nagkainan sila`` admits both ``EAT <SUBJ>`` and
  ``EAT-TOGETHER <SUBJ>`` readings); tests assert the
  reciprocal reading is among the n-best.
- **3-arg plain DV (CAUS=NONE) ditransitives** — lifted in
  Phase 5e Commit 11. Mirrors the multi-GEN-NP shape from
  Phase 5b (pa-OV) and Phase 5e Commit 10 (pa-DV) but for
  plain non-causative DV. New intrinsic profile
  ``_DV_TR_AGENT_PATIENT_RECIPIENT_THREE_ARG``, new sulat DV
  3-arg BASE entry with PRED ``WRITE-TO <SUBJ, OBJ-AGENT,
  OBJ-PATIENT>``, three new top-level multi-GEN-NP grammar
  rules for ``V[VOICE=DV, CAUS=NONE]``. ``Sinulatan ng nanay
  ng liham ang anak`` "Mother wrote a letter to the child" and
  the two NP-order permutations parse. Other DV anchor verbs
  (kain, basa, inom) typically use pa-DV for 3-arg readings;
  the plain-DV 3-arg pattern is sulat-only for now.
- **3-arg pa-DV (with overt PATIENT)** — lifted in Phase 5e
  Commit 10. Mirrors the Phase 5b 3-arg pa-OV-direct shape but
  with the pa-DV pivot (LOCATION) at SUBJ instead of CAUSEE.
  New intrinsic profile ``_DV_CAUS_DIRECT_THREE_ARG``, three
  new BASE entries (kain / basa / inom), three new top-level
  multi-GEN-NP grammar rules, two new ``S_XCOMP`` rules for
  3-arg pa-DV under control, plus extensions to
  ``S_GAP_OBJ_CAUSER`` (3-arg pa-DV CAUSER-fronting) and
  ``S_GAP_OBJ_PATIENT`` (3-arg pa-DV PATIENT-fronting).
  ``Pinakainan ng nanay ng kanin ang bata`` "Mother fed rice
  to the child"; ``Gusto kong pakakainan ang bata ng kanin``
  "I want to feed rice to the child"; ``Ng nanay ay
  pinakainan ang bata ng kanin`` etc.
- **Nested-control composition with multi-arg embedded clauses**
  — pinned in Phase 5e Commit 9. Two compositions that were
  structurally available via Phase 5c §7.6 Commit 3 (nested
  S_XCOMP rules) plus Phase 5d Commits 8 / 9 (pa-OV and IV
  multi-GEN at S_XCOMP level) but had no explicit test
  coverage: (a) **nested pa-OV under control** (3-level chain
  with the innermost being a pa-OV CAUS=DIRECT clause; the
  controller chains through SUBJ slots to the embedded
  ``OBJ-CAUSER``); (b) **IV multi-GEN under nested control**
  (3-level chain with 3-arg IV-BEN innermost; controller
  chains to ``OBJ-AGENT`` while ``OBJ-PATIENT`` stays overt as
  a GEN-NP). Both verified with explicit Python-id assertions
  on the cross-level f-node identity. Test-only commit.
- **Control / raising composition pinning** — pinned in
  Phase 5e Commit 8. Two compositions that were structurally
  available via Phase 5c §7.6 Commit 5 (raising at S level)
  plus Phase 5d Commit 7 (raising at S_XCOMP level) but had
  no explicit test coverage: (a) **control under raising**
  (``Mukhang gusto ng batang kumain``) where a raising matrix
  embeds a control clause as its inner S, and (b) **TRANS
  control + raising** (``Pinilit ng nanay ang batang mukhang
  umuwi``) where a TRANS control verb's XCOMP is itself a
  raising-S_XCOMP. Both compositions verified with explicit
  Python-id assertions on shared SUBJ slots; negation composes
  at any level. Test-only commit (no grammar / lex changes).
- **Resumptive pronouns in RCs (reclassification)** — Phase 5e
  Commit 7 documentation-only commit. Originally listed as a
  Phase 5e Group B candidate (rare in modern Tagalog);
  investigation showed Tagalog uses voice alternation, not
  resumptive pronouns, as the canonical non-SUBJ
  relativization strategy. Reclassified to plan §18
  v1-out-of-scope. No grammar / lex / morph changes.
- **`na` linker disambiguation after PRON** — lifted in
  Phase 5e Commit 6. The placement-pass disambiguator
  (``disambiguate_homophone_clitics``) gains a third
  left-context exception: when a PRON preceded by a NOUN
  (NP-internal possessor) is followed by a VERB (or a
  PART[POLARITY=NEG] then VERB), the post-PRON ``na`` stays as
  the linker rather than being moved to clause-end as the 2P
  aspectual clitic. A small look-ahead helper
  ``_next_content_is_verb`` skips negation tokens. This lifts
  Phase 5d Commit 10's deferral for sentences like ``Tumakbo
  ang bata ko na kumain`` "My child who ate ran." (Hand-
  authored AV-intransitive verbs only — the bare-V completeness
  trip on TR-only synthesizer-fallback verbs like ``nakita`` is
  the same engineering follow-up flagged in Commit 5.)
- **Headless / free relatives** — lifted in Phase 5e Commit 5.
  Three additive grammar rules
  (``NP[CASE=NOM] → DET[CASE=NOM, DEM=NO] S_GAP``, GEN/DAT
  variants with ADP) plus PRED='PRO' on the headless head and
  REL-PRO PRED='PRO' inheriting CASE. The DEM=NO constraint
  prevents cross-firing with the standalone-demonstrative rule
  (Phase 4 §7.8). What works: AV-INTR-RC
  (``Tumakbo ang tumakbo``), AV-tr-RC with overt OBJ
  (``Tumakbo ang kumain ng isda``), OV-tr-RC patient-pivot
  (``Tumakbo ang kinain ng aso``), headless RC in OBJ position
  (``Nakita ko ang tumakbo``), inner-NEG composition. Bare
  AV-tr headless without overt OBJ requires a hand-authored
  intransitive BASE entry — the synthesizer fallback emits
  only ``<SUBJ, OBJ>`` for TR-marked AV verbs, deferred as a
  separate engineering follow-up.
- **Multi-pronoun RC composition** — lifted in Phase 5e Commit 4.
  Test-only commit pinning the composition of a matrix-cluster
  PRON and an embedded RC-actor PRON in one sentence
  (``Nakita ko ang batang kinain niya``). Phase 5d Commit 10's
  ``_is_post_embedded_v_pron`` Wackernagel exception already
  handled per-token placement; this commit adds explicit
  assertions that both PRONs end up in distinct f-structure
  slots, the matrix and RC PRED values are distinct, and inner
  negation under either clause composes. Pronouns identified
  by structural position plus CASE / NUM (PERS rides as int and
  is dropped by the lex-equation derivation; exposing PERS as
  string is a follow-up).
- **AdvP / PP ay-fronting** — lifted in Phase 5e Commit 3. Two
  new POS values seeded in particles.yaml (``ADV`` for temporal
  adverbs kahapon/ngayon/bukas/mamaya; ``PREP`` for compound
  preposition heads para/tungkol/mula/dahil), two new
  non-terminals (``AdvP → ADV`` and ``PP → PREP NP[CASE=DAT]``),
  and two new ay-fronting wrap rules
  (``S → AdvP PART[LINK=AY] S``, ``S → PP PART[LINK=AY] S``)
  that put the fronted phrase into both ``TOPIC`` and ``ADJ``.
  The analyzer auto-adds ``LEMMA`` to ADV / PREP feats so the
  head-lemma percolates to the matrix TOPIC. Non-fronted
  AdvP / PP placement (clause-final or unmarked sentential
  adjunct) and multi-word AdvPs (``kahapon ng umaga``) remain
  deferred — the bare-form case is more frequent than the
  ay-fronted form and is a real residual gap, kept as a separate
  commit for review-scope reasons.
- **Multi-GEN-NP ay-fronting** — lifted in Phase 5e Commit 2.
  Phase 5b 3-arg multi-GEN frames have two GEN-NPs typed as
  ``OBJ-AGENT`` + ``OBJ-PATIENT`` (IV) or ``OBJ-CAUSER`` +
  ``OBJ-PATIENT`` (pa-OV-direct) positionally; fronting one of
  them needs the other GEN-NP's binding to be voice-conditioned
  rather than positional. Phase 5e Commit 2 adds two pieces:
  (a) ``S_GAP_OBJ_AGENT`` 3-arg IV variants for AGENT-fronting
  with retained OBJ-PATIENT; (b) a new ``S_GAP_OBJ_PATIENT``
  non-terminal for PATIENT-fronting (retained OBJ-AGENT under
  IV, retained OBJ-CAUSER under pa-OV-direct). Both NP orders
  per voice family. The 3-arg pa-OV CAUSER-fronting case was
  already handled by Phase 5e Commit 1's ``S_GAP_OBJ_CAUSER``
  3-arg rules. DV 3-arg multi-GEN ay-fronting still deferred
  (blocked on the DV multi-GEN rules themselves; Group D item).
- **pa-OV / pa-DV (CAUS=DIRECT) actor-fronting** — lifted in
  Phase 5e Commit 1. New gap-category ``S_GAP_OBJ_CAUSER``
  parallels Phase 5d Commit 5's ``S_GAP_OBJ_AGENT`` but routes
  REL-PRO to the typed ``OBJ-CAUSER`` slot instead of
  ``OBJ-AGENT``. Frames cover 2-arg pa-OV (with optional DAT
  adjunct), 3-arg pa-OV in both NP orders (overt OBJ-PATIENT
  retained), and 2-arg pa-DV. The wrap rule
  ``S → NP[CASE=GEN] PART[LINK=AY] S_GAP_OBJ_CAUSER`` shares
  the GEN-fronted shape with ``S_GAP_OBJ_AGENT``; CAUS feature
  on the inner V (DIRECT vs NONE) picks the right gap-category
  with no cross-firing. 3-arg pa-DV actor-fronting still
  deferred (lex profile is 2-arg only).
- **Pronominal RC-actor (in-place Wackernagel)** — lifted in
  Phase 5d Commit 10. Standard relativization with a pronominal
  actor in the RC (`Tumakbo ang batang kinain ko`) historically
  failed because the §7.3 Wackernagel pass hoisted `ko` from its
  RC-actor position into the matrix V's post-V cluster. New
  helper `_is_post_embedded_v_pron` keeps a PRON in place when it
  follows a non-first VERB (= an embedded V), distinguishing it
  from regular post-matrix-V cluster placement (preceding V IS
  the first V). OV / DV / IV-RC produce a unique RC reading;
  AV-RC produces two readings (RC-with-OBJ and head-possessor)
  preserved as competing parses. `na` linker disambiguation after
  PRON still deferred (`bata ko na nakita` style).
- **IV applicative multi-GEN under control** — lifted in Phase 5d
  Commit 9. Two changes to `S_XCOMP` IV rules: (a) drop the
  APPL=CONVEY filter on the existing 2-arg rule so IV-BEN /
  IV-INSTR / IV-REASON / IV-CONVEY all fire (the controllee is
  uniformly OBJ-AGENT under Phase 5b OBJ-θ-in-grammar);
  (b) add 3-arg variants `V[VOICE=IV] NP[CASE=NOM] NP[CASE=GEN]`
  in both NP orders for multi-GEN-NP applicatives under control.
  Phase 5b's 3-arg multi-GEN composes with the matrix control
  wrap rule's `(↑ SUBJ) = (↑ XCOMP REL-PRO)` to make the matrix
  controller bind the embedded OBJ-AGENT (the gap). IV-REASON
  CTPL paradigm gap still deferred (CTPL form analyses as
  IV-CONVEY; tests use PFV).
- **pa-OV / pa-DV (CAUS=DIRECT) under control** — lifted in
  Phase 5d Commit 8. Four new `S_XCOMP` rules route REL-PRO to
  the typed `OBJ-CAUSER` slot (the actor in monoclausal direct
  causatives) instead of `OBJ-AGENT`. Covers PSYCH / INTRANS /
  TRANS control with both 2-arg and 3-arg pa-OV (NOM-GEN and
  GEN-NOM orders), plus 2-arg pa-DV. Embedded form is the CTPL
  aspect (`pakakainin`, `pakakainan`) per Phase 4 §7.7 paradigms.
  3-arg pa-DV under control still deferred (lex profile is 2-arg
  only).
- **Raising chains + raising under control** — lifted in Phase 5d
  Commit 7. Raising chains (mukha-baka, parang-tila, etc.) already
  composed at the `S` level via the existing recursive raising
  rules; this commit pins the behaviour with explicit tests.
  Raising under control adds `S_XCOMP`-level raising variants so a
  control verb's complement can be a raising matrix
  (`Gusto kong mukhang kumakain`); the chain composes naturally to
  4-level XCOMP nesting (`Gusto kong mukhang bakang kumakain`)
  with all SUBJ slots structurally identical.
- **Possessive-linker RC variant** (`aklat kong binasa`) — lifted
  in Phase 5d Commit 6. The pronominal actor of a non-AV RC is
  hoisted out and surfaces as a possessor of the head NP, joined
  by the bound `-ng` linker. Three additive pieces: a Wackernagel
  pre-linker check (`_is_pre_linker_pron`) keeps the split PRON
  adjacent to its `-ng`; new gap-category `S_GAP_NA` for
  SUBJ-gapped non-AV V with no overt GEN-actor; new wrap rule
  `NP → NP PRON[GEN] PART[LINK=NG] S_GAP_NA` with dual-binding
  equations making POSS and the RC's OBJ-AGENT the same
  f-structure (id-identity). Vowel-final pronouns only (ko / mo /
  niya); consonant-final pronouns with standalone `na` linker
  remain deferred.
- **Non-pivot ay-fronting** (OBJ-θ in any voice + DAT-marked
  obliques) — lifted in Phase 5d Commit 5. Three new gap-category
  non-terminals (`S_GAP_OBJ`, `S_GAP_OBJ_AGENT`, `S_GAP_OBL`)
  parallel `S_GAP` with their own REL-PRO bindings; three new
  wrap rules per fronted case (GEN-AV, GEN-non-AV, DAT). The
  inner V's voice / features select the right gap-category
  alongside the fronted NP's case marker. AdvP / PP fronting
  remains deferred (no AdvP / PP categorial inventory yet); IV
  applicatives admitted without APPL constraint so CONVEY /
  INSTR / REASON all support actor-fronting.

- **OV / DV / IV control complements** with non-SUBJ gap — lifted
  in Phase 5c §7.6 follow-on. The embedded actor's typed GF
  (`OBJ-AGENT`) is now the gap-binding target for non-AV embedded
  clauses; psych and transitive control compose with OV / DV / IV
  embedded.
- **Long-distance (nested) control** through chained XCOMPs —
  lifted in Phase 5c §7.6 follow-on Commit 3. Six new S_XCOMP
  rules let a control verb be the head of an embedded clause;
  finite-depth chains compose without functional uncertainty.
  Long-distance *relativization* (the entry above) genuinely
  needs functional uncertainty and remains deferred.
- **`ipang-` instrumental and `ika-` reason applicatives** —
  lifted in Phase 5c §7.7 follow-on Commit 4. New
  `nasal_assim_prefix` sandhi op handles homorganic-nasal place-
  assimilation with consonant retention (`pang-` + `bili` →
  `pambili`, distinct from the existing drop pattern of
  `nasal_substitute`). 6 new IV cells (3 INSTR, 3 REASON) +
  4 lex entries; new `REASON` role in the Role enum.
- **Raising verbs** (`mukha` "seem", `baka` "might") — lifted in
  Phase 5c §7.6 follow-on Commit 5. PRED-template format
  extended with non-thematic args (``SEEM <XCOMP> SUBJ``); new
  ``CTRL_CLASS=RAISING`` lex entries; matrix wrap rule
  ``S → V[CTRL_CLASS=RAISING] PART[LINK] S`` with structure-
  shared SUBJ. Noun homonymy (``mukha`` "face", ``baka`` "cow")
  resolves via syntactic-position constraints — no pre-parse
  disambiguation needed.
- **Multi-OBL semantic disambiguation** — lifted in Phase 5c §8
  follow-on Commit 6. Each NP's f-structure now carries its
  head-noun ``LEMMA`` (percolated via N / NP rules); the
  classifier consults small lemma → semantic-class tables
  (PLACE, ANIMATE) and prefers semantic match over positional
  for multi-OBL cases. ``sa bata sa eskwela`` and ``sa eskwela
  sa bata`` produce the same f-structure (bata → OBL-RECIP,
  eskwela → OBL-LOC). Positional remains the fallback for
  same-class or unknown-lemma sa-NPs.
- **Bare-raising verbs** (``parang`` "seems like", ``tila``
  "apparently") — lifted in Phase 5d Commit 1. New
  ``CTRL_CLASS=RAISING_BARE`` value distinguishes them from
  the linker-taking ``mukha`` / ``baka``; new wrap rule
  ``S → V[CTRL_CLASS=RAISING_BARE] S`` admits the no-linker
  form. The CTRL_CLASS split prevents cross-firing on
  ``mukhang`` / ``bakang`` sentences via the parser's
  non-conflict feature matcher. ``yata`` continues as a
  Wackernagel 2P clitic, not a raising verb.
- **`pa-...-an` DV causative** — lifted in Phase 5d Commit 2.
  Three new DV cells under affix-class ``pa_an`` (parallel to
  the existing ``pa_in`` OV cells with ``-an`` suffix) plus
  lex entries for ``kain`` / ``inom`` / ``basa``. The
  voice_specs entry for plain DV gained an explicit CAUS=NONE
  constraint; a new DV CAUS=DIRECT entry routes the
  GEN-CAUSER to ``OBJ-CAUSER``. PRED template:
  ``CAUSE-EAT-AT <SUBJ, OBJ-CAUSER>`` etc. (LOCATION /
  recipient is the pivot).
- **Post-modifier demonstrative with linker** (``ang batang
  ito``) — lifted in Phase 5d Commit 3. Six new grammar rules
  (3 cases × 2 linker variants) admit ``NP[CASE=X]
  → NP[CASE=X] PART[LINK] DET/ADP[CASE=X, DEM=YES]`` with
  case agreement enforced at the parser level. The matrix
  shares the head NP's f-structure via ``(↑) = ↓1`` and
  copies the demonstrative's DEIXIS via
  ``(↑ DEIXIS) = ↓3 DEIXIS``.
- **Pre-modifier demonstrative with linker** (``itong bata``)
  — lifted in Phase 5e Commit 16. Six new grammar rules
  (3 cases × 2 linker variants) admit ``NP[CASE=X]
  → DET/ADP[CASE=X, DEM=YES] PART[LINK] N``. The dem
  replaces the case marker (rather than coexisting with one),
  so the matrix shares the dem's f-structure via ``(↑) = ↓1``
  (CASE / MARKER / DEIXIS percolate) and projects PRED + LEMMA
  from N. PROX dems take the bound ``-ng`` linker; MED / DIST
  take the standalone ``na`` linker. A fifth left-context
  exception in ``disambiguate_homophone_clitics`` keeps the
  MED / DIST ``na`` as a linker rather than the aspectual
  ``ALREADY`` 2P enclitic. (The disambiguator branch was
  buggy as shipped — see Phase 5e Commit 17 below.)
- **Demonstrative on a relativized head** (``ang batang ito
  na kumain``) — lifted in Phase 5e Commit 17. No new grammar
  rules; the existing Phase 5d Commit 3 post-mod-dem rule and
  Phase 4 §7.5 RC rule already compose, but the Phase 5e
  Commit 16 disambiguator branch (above) compared boolean
  ``DEM`` against the string ``"YES"`` and never fired,
  letting the clitic-pass move the standalone ``na`` to
  clause-final and breaking the surface order. Phase 5e
  Commit 17 fixes the comparison to ``DEM is True``;
  dem-on-RC parses correctly across all three deixis values
  with both linker variants. The Phase 5e Commit 16 MED /
  DIST cases now use the proper pre-mod-dem rule rather than
  the bare-NP fallback they had been silently riding on.
- **Multi-GEN-NP IV-INSTR / IV-REASON 3-arg frames** —
  lifted in Phase 5d Commit 4. Lex-only commit: four new
  3-arg lex entries (bili / tahi IV-INSTR; kain / sulat
  IV-REASON) plus two intrinsic profiles. The Phase 5b
  multi-GEN-NP grammar rules (voice-restricted to IV
  without APPL constraint) already admit these constructions;
  only lex was missing.
- **Pronominal possessive** (`ang libro ko`, `ng aklat niya`,
  etc.) — lifted in Phase 5c §7.8 follow-on. Context-aware
  Wackernagel placement keeps a PRON-clitic in place when it
  follows a NOUN; the existing NP-internal possessive rule then
  binds it as `POSS`.
- **Multi-GEN-NP applicative / causative frames** — lifted
  incrementally in Phase 5b §7.7 follow-on for IV-BEN and
  pa-OV-direct frames; remaining variants still deferred.
- **Pre-NP partitive quantifier** (`lahat ng bata`) — lifted in
  Phase 5b §7.8 follow-on (flat 3-child rule, no QP non-terminal).

## How to regenerate

```sh
# Regenerate the corpus YAML from the templates
hatch run python scripts/generate_coverage_corpus.py

# Run the harness
hatch run test tests/tgllfg/test_coverage_phase4.py
```

The harness asserts each sentence's expected outcome individually
and a 70% floor on overall parse-rate via
`test_coverage_summary`. Extending the corpus is a matter of
adding to the templates in
`scripts/generate_coverage_corpus.py` or the hand-authored
"classic" list at the bottom.
